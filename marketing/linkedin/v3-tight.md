At Finergy we build software for banks. Almost every AI case in those conversations lands on the same two questions: how do you stop the model from drifting while it works, and how do you keep control over the quality of what it produces.

They're the right questions, and most AI tooling can't answer them. The tools are built to produce — assistants, copilots, harnesses, faster hands attached to your attention. You get more output and less certainty. Look at a finished piece of agent work and you can't see who formulated it, who checked it, who accepted the risk, or what it cost.

So the bottleneck moved. Not producing the work — governing it.

That's what I built Qroky to solve. It treats every piece of agent work as a product: a consumer, a definition of done, a budget, a verification plan. Whoever specifies it doesn't execute it. Whoever executes it never accepts their own work. Verification is blind: a different agent gets the criteria and the result, never the reasoning. Bankers will recognize maker-checker.

That answers both questions. Drift gets caught because an independent agent checks the result against the criteria, not the story of how it was made. Quality stays controllable because "done" is defined before the work starts, and a human accepts the risk at the gate.

One person plus Qroky works like a company: you supply intent, the information only you have, and risk acceptance at the gates. Everything else runs as governed, verified, recorded production. Built for founders and regulated teams — not for everyone yet.

Open source, plain markdown and git, runs on your own machine. No servers to depend on.

If you already run agents on work that matters: who actually checks their output before you ship it?
