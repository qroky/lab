Agents can now produce almost anything you ask for. Research, specs, code, contracts in draft, a whole plan. What they can't produce is the confidence that the work is actually done right.

Everyone is shipping faster hands — assistants, copilots, harnesses. You get more output and less certainty. Look at any piece of agent work and you can't see who formulated it, who checked it, who accepted the risk, or what it cost. For a solo founder that's a quiet stress. For a bank it's a hard stop.

So the bottleneck moved. Not producing the work — governing it.

That's the problem I built Qroky to solve. It treats every piece of agent work as a product: a consumer, a definition of done, a budget, a verification plan. Whoever specifies it does not execute it. Whoever executes it never accepts their own work. Verification is blind — a different agent gets the criteria and the result, never the reasoning. Bankers will recognize maker-checker. Every decision is recorded, verbatim.

One person plus Qroky works like a company. You supply three things: intent, the information only you have, and risk acceptance at the gates. Everything else runs as governed, verified, recorded production. It's for founders who want to operate like a team, and for regulated teams who need an audit trail and recorded risk acceptance as architecture, not as an add-on.

What I did not build: humans out of the loop. The gate where you say go, no-go, or pivot stays. That is the point, not a limitation. And it is not a framework for everyone yet — founders and regulated first.

It's open source, plain markdown and git, and it runs on your own machine. No servers to depend on. The framework built and verified its own rulebook in the open, and the full record is public.

If you're already running agents: who actually checks their work before you ship it? That is the question Qroky is built around.
